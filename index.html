The Scientific Review

Direct Connection to Physics: While Denis Villeneuve's Arrival is widely celebrated for its focus on linguistics, the foundational framework of the entire plot is rooted directly in advanced classical mechanics—specifically, the contrast between Newtonian causal physics and Variational teleological principles (Lagrangian mechanics).

What the Film Gets Right: The film relies beautifully on a real-world mathematical quirk found in Fermat’s Principle of Least Time. When calculating how a beam of light refracts through water, the mathematics state that light will always choose the path that takes the absolute least amount of time. Philosophically, for a ray of light to choose the fastest path, it mathematically acts as if it "knows" its final destination before it even starts moving. Arrival takes this exact physical premise and brilliantly applies it to alien consciousness. The Heptapods do not experience the universe sequentially (Cause $\rightarrow$ Effect); they experience it simultaneously, mimicking variational principles where the start and end of a path are determined all at once. The math behind this branch of physics is 100% accurate and taught in modern universities.

The Inaccuracies & Sci-Fi Stretches: Where the science veers into science fiction is how this physics translates to psychology. The film relies heavily on the "strong form" of the Sapir-Whorf linguistic hypothesis, claiming that learning the alien language can physically rewire a human brain to break the thermodynamic arrow of time. In reality, human brains are bound by biology and entropy; learning a non-linear language cannot grant us precognition or allow us to violate the sequential nature of time. Additionally, the ship's manipulation of gravity (shifting it by 90 degrees inside the entry tunnel) requires a mastery of localized spacetime curvature that defies our current understanding of energy requirements, effectively acting as high-tech magic.

Conclusion: Overall, Arrival handles its science with immense respect. Instead of inventing fake technobabble, it takes a genuine, profound mathematical mystery about how our universe operates and turns it into an unforgettable narrative.
